# Sources & References

## Ancient Sources

### Primary Greek & Roman Texts
- Homer. *Odyssey*, circa 8th century BCE. (Book 9: Polyphemus episode, most famous Cyclops story)
- Hesiod. *Theogony*, circa 8th century BCE. (Lines 139-146, 501-506: Divine Cyclopes as craftsmen)
- Euripides. *Cyclops*, circa 408 BCE. (Only surviving complete Greek satyr play, retells Polyphemus story)
- Callimachus. *Hymn to Artemis*, 3rd century BCE. (References to Cyclopes)
- Apollonius of Rhodes. *Argonautica*, 3rd century BCE. (Cyclopes forging for Hephaestus)
- Theocritus. *Idylls*, 3rd century BCE. (Idyll 11: Polyphemus in love with Galatea)
- Virgil. *Aeneid*, 29-19 BCE. (Book 3: Aeneas encounters Cyclopes in Sicily)
- Ovid. *Metamorphoses*, 8 CE. (Book 13: Polyphemus and Galatea)
- Apollodorus. *Bibliotheca*, 1st-2nd century CE. (Comprehensive mythological accounts)
- Pausanias. *Description of Greece*, 2nd century CE. (Various references)

### Ancient Art
- Greek black-figure and red-figure pottery (6th-4th century BCE)
- Numerous vase paintings of Odysseus and Polyphemus
- Roman mosaics and frescoes
- Pompeian frescoes depicting Cyclopes
